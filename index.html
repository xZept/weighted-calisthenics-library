The Library of Weighted Calisthenics Exercises

This library contains everything you need to know about weighted calisthenics! A huge library of weighted calisthenics exercises from compounds to isolation movements, including their description and proper execution. A guide on how to integrate each exercises to your routine us included as well.

Six main compound movements

Photo by Edoardo Cuoghi on Unsplash

A vertical pushing movement great for developing the triceps, lower chest, and the front delts. Although pike pushups are better for targeting the shoulder, dips also gets the job done. Plus, it is easier to add weight.

Photo by Remy Mock from Kensui

A horizontal pushing movement that targets the upper chest, triceps, and front delts. Personally, I prefer doing incline push-ups on rings at a decline to emphasize the upper chest. This compliments the dips.

Photo by Austin Dunham from YouTube

A vertical pulling movement that targets the width of whole back, forearms, and biceps. One of the best pulling movements that will literally give you wings. Even the bodyweight only variation is challenging enough for newbie to intermediate athletes!

Photo by Muscle & Strength from YouTube

A horizontal pulling movement that targets the thickness of the back, biceps, and forearms. This movement grearly compliments weighted pullups. Contrary to the pull-ups, I prefer doin these for high volume.

Photo by Pavigym Prama on Unsplash

A squat movement that targets the quads. Even though I don't recommend overloading them, this movement is the largest contributor to my leg development. The balance demand will ensure that you are mechanically disadvantaged, providing sufficient stimulus for quad development.

Photo from the website TRAINHEROIC
